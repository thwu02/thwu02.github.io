A Year of Rain

The coldest days in spring are when it rains—  
Listening to a wistful tune of teardrops  
With its harmony of birdsong  
A step outside to feel  
Cold showers wake the world  
The warmest days in spring are when it rains  
Welcoming the perfume of violets and lilacs  

Summer sun's melody resolves  
To dissonant evening thunder  
A brief respite in the muggy air  

The patter carries with it a song—  
A march to minor from major  
Raindrops clinging to orange and red  

The warmest days in winter are when it rains—  
Falling through gaps in barren branches  
Washing the streets clean of salt  
A step outside to feel  
The violent drumming of teardrops  
The coldest days in winter are when it rains  
Extinguishing the warmth of melancholy
